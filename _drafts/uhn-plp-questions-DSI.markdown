1. I know Gary in your team is in charge of operating the Animal Resource Center System, not the data part. So, the people who work in the implementation part are managing some projects or programs and the people who work in the data science part involved in this data science program?

From what I understand, there is no clear separation between “Data” & “Implementation”, all the programs and projects have some level of data involved, and some level of change that needs to be implemented. Andre’s program is focused on building data science capabilities for all the UHN, but other teams apply data science in their work as well.


2. Can you explain to me about data governance detail?

Data governance is the ownership (who is legally accountable for the data) and stewardship (who manages the data every day), and the work of the Data Governance Project is to clarify the rules and responsibilities around managing, accessing, and generated insights from UHN’s data.


3. Are there some tools or applications to manage data quality?

Every business area has their own way to manage data quality. There is no standard tool being used at UHN.

4. Does your team develop application to machine-learning or data mining by in-house?
As far as I know, the Data Science Program team has been using commercial software to perform advanced data analytics.  We build our own machine learning algorithms and statistical prediction models for projects within our Data Science Program. We also have been using commercial software to build simulations. There are other teams at UHN who may develop machine learning algorithms/applications in other ways. Each team  collects its own data based on requirements, but we also have an Enterprise Data Warehouse Team and Decision Support team, amongst others,  that help with mining the data.
